Optical and Electronic Transport Properties of Monolayer Semiconductors under Strain and Gate Doping
Transition metal dichalcogenides possess rich excitonic physics. In this study, we fabricated high-quality devices encapsulated by hexagonal boron nitride. For instance,we found that et al。 reported similar results. Specifically, we investigated the excitation behavior at cryogenic temperature.
The optoelectronic response was systematically analyzed. mw power was adjusted from 1 mW to 10 MW. When adjusting the carrier density, the optical properties undergo dramatic modifications. The range was from 10–300K, and density was 5.2x10^12 cm-2. The backgate voltage was scanned to control doping.
Under electrostatic gating, we observed exciton-polariton coupling at room temperature. This spectral weight shift directly reflects the exciton–trion redistribution. The solution containing H₂O and dissolved SiO₂ was analyzed. In addition, the thickness of Al2O3 layer determines the gate capacitance.
The critical energy spacing is determined by gate voltages. The density is measured in units of cm⁻³ or m⁻², and the energy is determined by Eg. For comparison, the intensity I varies with temperature as sin(x). The emission peaks are fitted by standard Lorentzian profiles, yielding the peak energy, full width at half maximum (FWHM), and integrated intensities. This was measured in situ.
Further experimental analysis was conducted. The data is consistent, and with the increase of temperature, the peak disappears. This behavior is well explained by the thermal dissociation model.
